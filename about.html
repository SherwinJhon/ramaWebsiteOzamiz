About Ozamiz

Nestled in the heart of Misamis Occidental in Northern Mindanao, Ozamiz City is a thriving urban center with a rich history and vibrant culture. Known as the "Gateway to Northwestern Mindanao," Ozamiz offers a harmonious blend of natural beauty, historical significance, and cultural traditions, making it a fascinating destination for both locals and tourists.

Historical background

Nestled in the heart of Misamis Occidental in Northern Mindanao, Ozamiz City is a thriving urban center with a rich history and vibrant culture. Known as the "Gateway to Northwestern Mindanao," Ozamiz offers a harmonious blend of natural beauty, historical significance, and cultural traditions, making it a fascinating destination for both locals and tourists.

Ozamiz Famous For

Ozamiz City is best known for its historical landmarks and religious significance. One of its most iconic sites is the Cotta Fort (Fuerte de la Concepción y del Triunfo), a Spanish-era fortress built in 1755 to protect the settlement from pirate attacks. Adjacent to the fort is the Birhen sa Cotta Shrine, a revered religious site featuring an image of the Virgin Mary believed to be miraculous. The annual Feast of Nuestra Señora de la Concepción y del Triunfo attracts pilgrims from across the country, highlighting the city’s deep-rooted faith and devotion.

Ozamiz is also famous for its picturesque natural attractions. The city serves as the gateway to the majestic Mount Malindang Range Natural Park, a UNESCO-declared ASEAN Heritage Park teeming with diverse flora and fauna. Adventure seekers flock to its waterfalls, including the serene Naomi's Botanical Garden and Resort and the stunning Hoyohoy Highland Stone Chapel Adventure Park, where visitors can enjoy breathtaking views and thrilling activities like ziplining. This mix of history, culture, and nature makes Ozamiz a truly remarkable destination.
